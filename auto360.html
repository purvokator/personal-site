AUTO360

UI/UX - Mobile & Web Apps | Branding | 2020

Web and mobile applications for automotive enthusiasts,
this application provides all automotive services, from news, marketplaces and exhibitions.

Main Idea

When covid 19 hit, the entire exhibition was affected.
Entering the new normal period, we think that when the exhibition is running,
we have to adapt to this pandemic. As the organizer of the auto show,
we think that there must be a platform that can accommodate all exhibition activities and can also support
the currently declining automotive industry. The platform for all automotive enthusiasts.


How we started

We initiated the process using a Design Thinking methodology:
Discover, Define, Develop and Deliver.

Stage 1: Discover

We began with a qualitative investigation, doing unstructured interviews (15 in total)
to understand the context and validate the assumptions we made about our users.


Unstructured interviews

This tool is perfect for the exploration stage. We needed first-hand information about our users to get to know their
profound motivations, opinions and perceptions about automotive marketplace & exhibition experience.

We designed questions about the following topics:
• Motivations, fears and expectations.
• Tools, channels used to buy automotive product.
• Tools, channels used to visit exhibition.
• What our users do during the exhibition show.
• What our users problem during buying automotive product online.

We found pains point & motivations of users.
Pains:
• Not accustomed to buying a car / motorcycle online
• Not comfortable with too many applications
• Buy a car must see the product

Motivations:
• Wish there was a platform that contained all automotive needs
• Buy a car from a trusted one
Thanks to the interviews, we discovered patterns and found that pains point & motivations

Stage 2: Define

Now that we had our target user, it was time to synthesize all the information we collected during the interviews.


Persona

We defined an archetype, that is a representation of a group of users, with their motivations, behaviours and fears.
This tool is designed to help us during all the design process to remember the users’ goals.


Design Challenge

How do you get Deni to trust and get used to buying automotive products in the online shop?
And how can we satisfy their automotive needs for exhibitions as well as shopping in one application?

Stage 3: Develop

We created solutions using paper prototyping. This method allowed us
to iterate the original idea and improve the product design faster at a low cost.

Stage 4: Deliver

We arrived at the final stage of the process. At this point, we already knew
the value proposition of our product, the main business objectives and our users’ needs and goals.

Wireframing and visual design
